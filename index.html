01 / About

Eleven years now turning messy data into stuff people actually use: dashboards, automated reports, models that predict things, and more recently, AI tools that flag problems and recommend next steps on their own, instead of just handing you a chart. At World Fuel Services I run BI for the whole Marine segment: 230+ dashboards and 35 automated workflows, feeding about 200 people across Sales, Finance, and Ops.

Before any of that I was a Sergeant and Fuel Team Lead in the Marine Corps, running fueling ops for 100+ aircraft and a 2.8-million-gallon fuel farm. Zero safety incidents the whole time I ran it, and I earned a Navy Achievement Medal for it.

Tech-wise I live in Snowflake, SQL, and Tableau for BI, Python/FastAPI on the backend, React and Streamlit for anything front-facing. These days most of my time goes into agentic pipelines, working with Claude Code, Copilot, Snowflake Cortex, Perplexity, and Codex most days. More recently I've been doing freelance work on the side too. I built and still run a Django app for a real meal-prep business that used to plan everything on spreadsheets. It handles their client meal plans, turns everyone's weekly orders into actual thaw and packing lists for the kitchen, runs on staff phones, and flags inventory before they run short. Solo build, still live, still their day-to-day.

Based in Florida, finishing a BS in Applied Math (minor in AI/Data Science) at FIU, and open to senior BI, analytics engineering, or AI automation roles if the right one comes along.
